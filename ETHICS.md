# Ethical Use

SciResearchKit is a productivity aid for scientific work. It helps a human researcher plan a study, draft prose, structure a review, and check citations more efficiently. It does not replace the researcher, and it does not remove any obligation the researcher already has to the venue, the funder, the institution, or the reader.

Use of this toolkit is conditional on the four rules below.

## 1. Human researcher stays in the loop

A qualified human is responsible for every output that leaves the researcher's workstation with their name on it.

Every deliverable produced with SciResearchKit must be read, verified, and signed off by that human before it is submitted, published, sent to a co-author, or used as a decision input. This applies to prose, tables, figures, citations, statistical claims, review comments, rebuttals, and grant text.

The toolkit's quality gates are pre-checks, not sign-offs. A passed gate does not certify correctness.

## 2. Respect venue and funder AI-use policies

Journals, conferences, funders, and institutions set their own rules on what AI assistance is allowed, how it must be disclosed, and where it is prohibited. Those rules take precedence over any convenience this toolkit offers.

Before using SciResearchKit on a specific deliverable, check and comply with:

- The target journal or conference policy on AI-assisted writing, review, and editing.
- Any explicit prohibition of AI in peer review or grant review (many venues and funders now ban submitting reviewer reports drafted by AI systems, on confidentiality and integrity grounds).
- The funder's terms on AI use in proposal preparation.
- The employer or institutional policy on AI use in research.
- Co-author consent when a shared manuscript passes through an AI system.

Where a policy requires disclosure, disclose. Where a policy prohibits use, do not use the toolkit for that task.

## 3. Confidentiality: do not upload material you do not have the right to share

Peer review, grant review, and unpublished manuscripts are confidential by default. Do not paste them into a hosted AI system unless you have explicit permission from the party that entrusted the material to you (the editor for a review assignment, the funder for a proposal, the co-authors for an unpublished draft, the patients or the ethics board for identifiable data).

Personally identifiable data, clinical data covered by consent forms or IRB/ethics approval, and third-party proprietary data must not be sent to any AI system that would transmit them outside the boundary the consent covers.

If in doubt, do not upload.

## 4. Prohibited uses

Do not use SciResearchKit to:

- Fabricate data, results, citations, participants, or outcomes.
- Generate a peer-review report or manuscript-review report that is submitted as if the human wrote it, in violation of a venue's AI-use policy for reviewers.
- Draft or submit any material for a venue whose policy prohibits AI assistance in that task.
- Bypass a required disclosure of AI assistance.
- Impersonate a co-author, reviewer, editor, or funder.
- Produce material that violates copyright, plagiarises, or misrepresents authorship.
- Circumvent institutional, funder, or legal restrictions on the use of AI in research activity.

## Disclosure suggestion

When the target venue's policy permits AI-assisted preparation with disclosure, the following short statement can serve as a starting point. Adapt it to the venue's specific wording:

> The authors used SciResearchKit (a Markdown research-writing skill loaded into an AI assistant) as an editing and structuring aid during preparation of this manuscript. All scientific content, claims, citations, and analysis decisions were verified by the authors. The authors take full responsibility for the manuscript as submitted.

## Summary

SciResearchKit is intended to raise the floor of first-draft quality and shorten mechanical checks. It is not a substitute for scientific judgment, expert review, or the researcher's accountability for the work.
