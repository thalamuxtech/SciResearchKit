# Grants and Proposals

Phase 8. Use this for specific aims, significance, proposals, and budget justification. A proposal sells a plan. Reviewers fund clear logic and feasible work.

## The logic chain a proposal must show

A funded proposal connects five links without a gap:

1. The problem matters.
2. A gap blocks progress.
3. Your aims close the gap.
4. Your approach is feasible and you can do it.
5. The result changes the field or practice.

If any link is weak, the proposal fails. Reviewers find the weak link first.

## Specific aims page

The one page that decides most applications. Structure:

1. Opening. The problem and the gap, with evidence. Three to four sentences.
2. The critical need. What is missing and why it blocks progress.
3. Long-term goal and the objective of this application.
4. Central hypothesis, with the basis for it.
5. The aims. Two or three, each a testable objective with a short approach.
6. Payoff. What changes when the work succeeds.

Rules:

- Aims are independent. A failure in Aim 1 does not sink Aim 2.
- Aims are objectives, not tasks. State what you will learn, not only what you will do.
- State the hypothesis for each aim.
- Match the scope to the budget and the timeline.

## Significance

- State the size and the cost of the problem with data.
- State what current approaches cannot do.
- State how your work moves the field, not only your lab.
- Tie significance to the funder's mission.

## Innovation

- State what is new: the concept, the method, the application, or the combination.
- Be specific and honest. Reviewers know the field.
- Distinguish incremental from substantial. Claim what the evidence supports.

## Approach

- For each aim: rationale, design, methods, expected outcomes, and alternatives.
- Include a power or feasibility estimate.
- Name the pitfalls and the fallback for each.
- Show preliminary data that support feasibility.
- Include a timeline and milestones.

## Rigor and reproducibility

Funders require a rigor statement. Address:

- The scientific premise and its strength.
- The design features that ensure rigor: blinding, randomization, sample size.
- Biological and other key variables, such as sex as a variable.
- Authentication of key resources.

## Budget justification

- Tie every line to an aim and a task.
- Justify personnel effort against the work.
- Justify equipment and the need.
- Keep the budget proportional to the scope.

## Writing style for proposals

- Apply `writing-style.md`. Short sentences. Active voice. No filler.
- Use white space and headers. Reviewers read many proposals under time pressure.
- Lead each section with its main point.
- Calibrate claims with `claims-and-language.md`. Do not overpromise outcomes.

## Quality gate for grants and proposals

1. The five-link logic chain holds with no weak link.
2. Aims are independent, testable objectives, each with a hypothesis.
3. Significance and innovation are specific and backed by data.
4. The approach names pitfalls and fallbacks and shows feasibility.
5. The rigor statement addresses premise, design, variables, and resources.
6. The budget ties to aims and matches the scope.
7. The writing follows the spartan style and calibrated claims.
